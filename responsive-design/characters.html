Eponine

Éponine Thénardier is the cynical but resourceful and street-smart eldest daughter and first-born child of Monsieur and Madame Thénardier. She lived a comfortable and spoiled childhood and throughout her teen and adult years she and her family are impoverished. Cosette stays with her family at their inn for the beginning of their childhoods and is treated poorly compared to Eponine, only for their situations to reverse in the coming years, with Cosette living a rather easy life while Eponine is poor and near starving. She falls in love with Marius, only for her love to be unrequited as he loves Cosette. This is another area where Cosette seems to have eveything Eponine wants.

Fantine

Fantine is a poor, working-class girl from the desolate seacoast town of Montreuil-sur-mer, an orphan who has almost no education and can neither read nor write. Fantine is inevitably betrayed by the people she does trust: Tholomyès gets her pregnant and then disappears; the Thénardiers take Cosette and use the child to extort more money; and Fantine’s coworkers have her fired for indecency. In his descriptions of Fantine’s life and death, Hugo highlights the unfair attitude of French society toward women and the poor. Fantine’s fellow citizens criticize her for her behavior and depravity, but they also take every opportunity to make her circumstances even more desperate.

Jean Val Jean

Jean Valjean is the protagonist of Victor Hugo's 1862 novel Les Misérables. The story depicts the character's 19-year-long struggle to lead a normal life after serving a prison sentence for stealing bread to feed his sister's children during a time of economic depression and various attempts to escape from prison. Valjean is also known in the novel as Monsieur Madeleine, Ultime Fauchelevent, Monsieur Leblanc, and Urbain Fabre. He is a parallel to Inspector Javear, and he is portrayed by Hugh Jackman in the movie.
