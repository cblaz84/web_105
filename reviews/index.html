HARRY POTTER AND THE SORCERER'S STONE

By: J.K. Rowling

Overview

Harry Potter is a lonely and sad orphan, mistreated and abused by his extended family. His “family” constantly reminds him that he does not really belong as part of their family and he feels completely alone and unwanted. That is until he learns he is actually a wizard!? On his twelfth birthday he is invited to attend Hogwarts, a school of wizardy. It is here that Harry’s story begins and where he finds where he truly belongs.

This story is not only about Harry finding where he belongs and living happily. He may find his new home a safe refuge from his old tormenting relatives, but new dangers await Harry at Hogwarts, and an ancient evil long thought gone seems to be awakening with Harry's return. Harry, alongside his new friends he finds at Hogwarts, must help protect his new home and defeat the rising evil.

“Harry Potter and the Sorcerer's Stone” is a charming and imaginative tale about a young boy finding where he belongs. The story follows the protagonist, Harry Potter, as he discovers a magical world and his place within it. From the wondrous world-building to the lovable characters, readers will thoroughly enjoy this novel at any age. Just from personal experience, I was able to connect with many of the characters and had an absolutely great time reading the book. I couldn't wait to get a hold of hold of the next book in the series.“It was a dream, I dreamed a giant called Hagrid came to tell me I was going to a school for wizards. When I open my eyes I’ll be at home in my cupboard.” (p. 61)

Harry Potter: the Series

This being only the first in J.K. Rowling’s long series that follows Harry, it serves as a proper introduction to this new world. The reader is able to properly integrate themselves and slowly grow accustomed to this new world as Harry also adjusts. The series as a whole is a beautiful tale of growth and friendship that all readers can appreciate and enjoy. It is critically acclaimed and it is quite rare to find someone who has not read the Harry Potter Series, or, at the very least, heard of them.
